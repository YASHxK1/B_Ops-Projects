# Correlation Matrix Based Marketing Strategy
### Statistical Analysis of Advertising Campaigns

## Why This project Stands Out

Most marketing analysis stops at surface-level metrics. This research goes deeper — using correlation analysis to map the actual statistical relationships between every major advertising KPI and identify where in the funnel optimization effort has the highest payoff.

The result is not just a data report. It is a decision-making framework built entirely on empirical evidence, applicable to any digital advertising operation looking to improve performance without simply increasing spend.

---

## What the Research Proves

**Spending more is not the answer.**
Budget shows zero correlation with impressions, clicks, conversions, CTR, CPC, and ROI across the entire dataset. This finding alone reframes how campaign budgets should be evaluated and allocated.

**The funnel is statistically validated.**
Impressions drive clicks (r = 0.726). Clicks drive conversions (r = 0.766). The sequential acquisition path holds up quantitatively, giving practitioners a reliable structure to optimize within.

**Clicks are the highest-leverage point in the funnel.**
They are responsive to upstream creative and targeting decisions, and they are the strongest single predictor of conversions. Optimizing here produces compounding gains across both funnel stages simultaneously.

**CTR is not a reliable success signal.**
The correlation between CTR and conversion rate is essentially zero (r = 0.005). This challenges one of the most commonly tracked metrics in digital advertising and redirects attention toward post-click experience quality.

**ROI cannot be explained by any single metric.**
Profitability operates independently from traffic volume, engagement rates, and cost efficiency. The research identifies this gap and points toward revenue-per-conversion and lifetime value modeling as the missing variables.
